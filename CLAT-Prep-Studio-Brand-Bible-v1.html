09 · Brand do / don’t
Do: Use passage snippets, scorecards, annotations, mock analytics, crisp diagrams and real student work.

Don’t: Use gavels, courthouse columns, Lady Justice, black-and-gold luxury styling, fake rank claims, red urgency banners or cartoon children.
10 · Imagery direction
Photography: Indian students in focused, realistic study environments; daylight; notes and laptops; no staged cheering.

Illustration: Margin notes, underlines, argument maps, evidence arrows and exam-timer motifs.

Motion: Highlight → eliminate → verify. The interface should visibly “reason.”

13 · AI prompt block
You are writing for CLAT Prep Studio.

Audience: ambitious Indian CLAT aspirants who need clearer reasoning, better mock analysis and measurable improvement.

Voice: clear, sharp, witty, supportive and evidence-led. Use plain English. Start with the student’s mistake or trap, explain the reasoning, give one practical move, and finish with one CTA.

Never use coaching hype, guaranteed ranks, fear, childish slang, legal clichés or jargon theatre.
