# Issue Dossier

## 1. What Happened
The Supreme Court delivered landmark constitutional rulings defining the precise timeframes within which State Governors must exercise options under Article 200 when presented with Bills passed by elected state legislatures.

## 2. Background
Historical tension over gubernatorial delays under Article 200, judicial interpretation in *Shamsher Singh* (1974), Sarkaria Commission (1983), and Punchhi Commission (2007).

## 3. Timeline
- **1974**: Shamsher Singh v. State of Punjab establishes Governor acts on Aid and Advice of Council of Ministers.
- **1983**: Sarkaria Commission recommends Governor assent within 6 months.
- **2023-2024**: SC rules in Punjab & Telangana petitions that 'as soon as possible' implies immediate action without indefinite delay.

## 7. CLAT Passage
The constitutional role of the Governor under Article 200 has emerged as a major flashpoint in Indian federalism. Article 200 provides four distinct courses of action when a Bill passed by the State Legislature is presented to the Governor: granting assent, withholding assent, returning the Bill for reconsideration (if not a Money Bill), or reserving the Bill for the President’s consideration under Article 201. The Constitution uses the phrase 'as soon as possible' for returning a Bill. In recent landmark decisions, the Supreme Court clarified that a Governor cannot withhold assent indefinitely without returning the Bill to the Assembly. If the Assembly passes the Bill again with or without amendment, the Governor is constitutionally bound to grant assent.

### Questions
1. **What happens if a State Legislative Assembly re-passes a returned non-Money Bill with or without amendments?**:
   - (A) The Governor must reserve it for the Prime Minister.
   - (B) The Governor is bound to grant assent under Article 200 proviso.
   - (C) The Bill automatically lapses.
   - (D) The Governor can dissolve the Assembly.
   - *Correct Answer*: B
   - *Explanation*: Under the proviso to Article 200, if the Assembly re-passes the returned Bill, the Governor shall not withhold assent.

## 8. AILET MCQs
1. **Which constitutional Article deals with the Governor reserving a State Bill for Presidential consideration?**:
   - (A) Article 161
   - (B) Article 200
   - (C) Article 201
   - (D) Article 213
   - *Correct Answer*: C
   - *Explanation*: Article 201 governs Bills reserved by the Governor for the consideration of the President.
